5. Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are provided to improve the RFP before publication:

5.1 High Priority Recommendations

  • Include detailed integration requirements for existing systems, including system names, versions, data formats, and integration points. This will help vendors understand the integration complexity and provide more accurate responses.
  • Add more detail on data migration expectations, including data volumes, quality, complexity, and validation requirements. Data migration is often one of the most complex and risky aspects of ERP implementation, so clear expectations are essential.

5.2 Medium Priority Recommendations

  • Add more specific technical requirements for each module, including data fields, workflows, and reporting needs. This will help vendors understand your specific requirements and provide more tailored solutions.
  • Define specific performance metrics and SLAs, including response times, throughput, availability, and recovery time objectives. This will ensure that vendors understand your performance expectations and can design their solutions accordingly.
  • Expand training and knowledge transfer requirements, including types of training, number of users, and knowledge transfer expectations. This will help vendors provide more accurate training proposals and ensure that your team is adequately prepared to use and maintain the system.
  • Include more information on current systems and data structures to help vendors understand the migration complexity. This will enable vendors to better assess the effort required for data migration and integration.
  • Specify testing requirements, including unit testing, integration testing, user acceptance testing, and performance testing. This will ensure that vendors include comprehensive testing in their proposals.

5.3 Low Priority Recommendations

  • Include change management expectations, including communication, training, and user adoption strategies. This will help vendors understand your expectations for managing organizational change during the implementation.
  • Consider adding more detail on reporting and analytics requirements, including specific reports, dashboards, and analytics capabilities. This will ensure that vendors understand your reporting needs and can provide appropriate solutions.
  • Provide more information on the expected user base, including the number of users, roles, and access requirements. This will help vendors size their solutions appropriately and provide more accurate licensing costs.
